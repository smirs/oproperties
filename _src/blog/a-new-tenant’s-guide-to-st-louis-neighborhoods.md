# A New Tenant’s Guide to St. Louis Neighborhoods

If you’re moving to St. Louis from out of state, you’re in for a treat. The Gateway to the West is a city filled with rich history, diverse communities, and plenty of charm. But like any city, choosing the right neighborhood is key to making your transition smooth and finding a place that truly feels like home. Whether you're drawn to lively nightlife, quiet suburban streets, or cultural vibrancy, St. Louis offers something for everyone.

This guide will help you navigate some of the best neighborhoods in St. Louis based on lifestyle, budget, and amenities. From family-friendly areas to urban hotspots, here’s everything you need to know about the neighborhoods that make this city unique.

- - -

## 1. **Central West End (CWE)**

### Overview:

The Central West End is one of the most vibrant and upscale neighborhoods in St. Louis. It’s known for its historic architecture, lively dining scene, and proximity to Forest Park, often referred to as the “heart” of the city.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Trendy, walkable, and filled with cultural landmarks.
* **Housing Options:** A mix of luxury apartments, historic homes, and modern condos.
* **Who It’s For:** Professionals, young couples, and anyone who enjoys urban living.

### Highlights:

* Forest Park: Home to the St. Louis Zoo, Art Museum, and plenty of outdoor activities.
* Euclid Avenue: Packed with cafes, restaurants, and boutiques.
* Nearby institutions like Barnes-Jewish Hospital and Washington University.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $1,200–$1,800/month.

- - -

## 2. **Soulard**

### Overview:

If you love history and a vibrant social scene, Soulard might be the perfect fit. As one of the oldest neighborhoods in St. Louis, it’s famous for its French-inspired architecture and the annual Mardi Gras celebration.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Energetic, community-oriented, and lively.
* **Housing Options:** Historic brick townhouses and apartments.
* **Who It’s For:** Social butterflies, history buffs, and those who enjoy nightlife.

### Highlights:

* Soulard Farmers Market: One of the oldest farmers' markets in the U.S.
* A thriving bar and live music scene.
* Annual Mardi Gras festivities.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $900–$1,400/month.

- - -

## 3. **The Hill**

### Overview:

The Hill is St. Louis’s Italian neighborhood, renowned for its authentic cuisine and close-knit community. It’s a charming area with a rich heritage and strong local pride.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Family-friendly, quiet, and steeped in tradition.
* **Housing Options:** Quaint single-family homes and duplexes.
* **Who It’s For:** Families and foodies.

### Highlights:

* Authentic Italian restaurants and bakeries like Gioia’s Deli and Rigazzi’s.
* A sense of community and a slower pace of life.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $800–$1,200/month.

- - -

## 4. **Clayton**

### Overview:

Clayton is the business hub of St. Louis County and is known for its upscale vibe. It’s home to top-rated schools, high-end dining, and a thriving job market.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Professional, polished, and family-oriented.
* **Housing Options:** Luxury apartments, modern townhomes, and single-family houses.
* **Who It’s For:** Executives, families, and anyone seeking a suburban feel with urban amenities.

### Highlights:

* Shaw Park: Great for outdoor activities and events.
* Downtown Clayton: Packed with upscale restaurants and boutiques.
* Proximity to top schools and corporate headquarters.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $1,500–$2,200/month.

- - -

## 5. **Tower Grove South**

### Overview:

A vibrant, diverse neighborhood with a mix of historic charm and modern amenities. Tower Grove South is known for its strong sense of community and access to green spaces.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Artsy, eclectic, and family-friendly.
* **Housing Options:** Historic homes, bungalows, and apartments.
* **Who It’s For:** Young families, artists, and professionals.

### Highlights:

* Tower Grove Park: A beautiful Victorian park that hosts events, markets, and festivals.
* South Grand Boulevard: A hub for international dining and shopping.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $800–$1,400/month.

- - -

## 6. **Lafayette Square**

### Overview:

One of the most picturesque neighborhoods in St. Louis, Lafayette Square is known for its Victorian-style homes and scenic park. It’s an ideal spot for those who appreciate historic charm.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Quiet, picturesque, and close-knit.
* **Housing Options:** Victorian townhouses and modern apartments.
* **Who It’s For:** Retirees, families, and professionals who love historic architecture.

### Highlights:

* Lafayette Park: The oldest park west of the Mississippi River.
* Local cafes, restaurants, and wine bars.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $1,200–$1,800/month.

- - -

## 7. **Maplewood**

### Overview:

Located just outside the city limits, Maplewood combines small-town charm with big-city convenience. It’s a popular choice for those seeking a relaxed atmosphere and affordable living.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Quirky, affordable, and family-friendly.
* **Housing Options:** Craftsman homes, apartments, and duplexes.
* **Who It’s For:** Families and young professionals.

### Highlights:

* Manchester Road: Known for its indie shops and eateries.
* Schlafly Bottleworks: A popular local brewery.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $900–$1,300/month.

- - -

## 8. **Downtown St. Louis**

### Overview:

For those who want to be in the center of it all, Downtown St. Louis offers a vibrant urban lifestyle. It’s home to iconic landmarks, sporting events, and a growing arts scene.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Fast-paced, urban, and convenient.
* **Housing Options:** Loft apartments, high-rise condos, and modern studios.
* **Who It’s For:** Young professionals and those who love city living.

### Highlights:

* Gateway Arch National Park.
* Busch Stadium: Home to the St. Louis Cardinals.
* City Museum: A unique blend of art and playground.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $1,000–$1,500/month.

- - -

## 9. **Kirkwood**

### Overview:

Kirkwood is a charming suburb known for its excellent schools, historic train station, and vibrant downtown area. It’s a top choice for families.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Quiet, family-oriented, and community-driven.
* **Housing Options:** Ranch-style homes, single-family houses, and modern apartments.
* **Who It’s For:** Families and retirees.

### Highlights:

* Kirkwood Farmers’ Market.
* The Magic House: A popular children’s museum.
* Quaint downtown shopping and dining.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $1,200–$1,800/month.

- - -

## 10. **The Grove**

### Overview:

The Grove is an up-and-coming neighborhood known for its LGBTQ+ friendly vibe, street art, and vibrant nightlife. It’s one of the city’s trendiest areas.

### Key Features:

* **Lifestyle:** Trendy, inclusive, and lively.
* **Housing Options:** Modern apartments, renovated homes, and townhouses.
* **Who It’s For:** Young professionals, creatives, and nightlife enthusiasts.

### Highlights:

* Manchester Avenue: Packed with bars, breweries, and restaurants.
* Murals and street art.
* Close to the Cortex Innovation District.

### Average Rent:

* 1-bedroom apartment: $1,000–$1,400/month.

## Things to Consider When Moving to St. Louis

### 1. **Transportation:**

St. Louis has a decent public transit system, including MetroLink trains and MetroBus services. However, many residents prefer driving due to the city’s layout. Be prepared to own a car if you’re moving here.

### 2. **Cost of Living:**

St. Louis is an affordable city compared to other metro areas. Housing, groceries, and transportation costs are all below the national average.

### 3. **Safety:**

Like any city, St. Louis has areas with higher crime rates. Research neighborhoods carefully, talk to locals, and visit during different times of the day to get a feel for safety and comfort.

### 4. **Climate:**

St. Louis experiences all four seasons. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters bring occasional snow. Pack for both extremes if you’re moving from a milder climate.

## Final Thoughts

St. Louis is a city with a neighborhood for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a quiet suburban retreat, a lively urban experience, or something in between, this guide should help you find the perfect fit.
